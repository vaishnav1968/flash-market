FlashMarket - Hackathon Project Summary
=======================================

1) One-line Pitch
FlashMarket is a real-time marketplace for surplus perishable food where prices automatically drop as expiry approaches, helping vendors reduce waste and helping buyers get affordable food deals.


2) Problem We Are Solving
- Local food vendors often throw away unsold perishable items at the end of the day.
- Buyers miss out on good food because they do not know what is available nearby in time.
- Traditional e-commerce is not optimized for short shelf-life products.


3) Our Solution
FlashMarket connects vendors and buyers through a live marketplace:
- Vendors list perishable items with quantity, shelf life, and base price.
- A dynamic pricing engine reduces price over time until expiry.
- Buyers can claim items quickly before they expire.
- Buyers can choose pickup or delivery (distance-based fee).
- Delivery opportunities can be claimed by nearby users on a map.


4) Core Features
A) Real-time Dynamic Pricing
- Price updates as time passes.
- Formula concept: price decays from base price to a floor price before expiry.
- Helps clear inventory faster and reduce food waste.

B) Vendor Dashboard
- Add and manage listings in seconds.
- Track live listings, sold-out count, and stock units.
- Vendor profile includes location for delivery quote support.

C) Buyer Marketplace
- Browse cards with live countdown and current discount.
- Search and category filters.
- Claim flow with quantity selection.

D) Claim Flow with Fulfillment Choice
- Option 1: Pickup (pay at vendor shop).
- Option 2: Delivery (fee based on distance).
- Delivery fee logic: distance_km x 11.

E) Nearby Delivery Map
- Shows available deliveries around user location.
- Claim and complete deliveries.
- Credit reward system for deliverers.

F) Authentication and Roles
- Google login via Supabase Auth.
- Role-based flow for buyer and vendor.
- Guest route support for non-logged-in users.


5) Tech Stack
- Frontend: Next.js 16 (App Router), React, Tailwind CSS, TypeScript
- Backend/API: Next.js API routes
- Database/Auth: Supabase (Postgres + Auth)
- Maps: react-map-gl / maplibre-gl
- Notifications: Resend-ready integration
- Deployment: Netlify with Next.js runtime plugin


6) System Design (Simple)
Client (Web/PWA)
  -> Next.js App UI (Marketplace, Vendor Dashboard, Delivery Map)
  -> Next.js API Routes (/api/items, /api/items/[id]/claim, /api/deliveries/*, /api/users/*)
  -> Supabase (users, items, claims, deliveries)

Key Data Tables:
- users: profile, role, location, credits
- items: listing details, price basis, status, expiry
- claims: buyer claims and payout split
- deliveries: delivery tasks and rewards


7) Why This Is Innovative for Hackathon
- Tackles food waste + affordability (social impact + business viability).
- Real-time urgency model (time-decay pricing).
- End-to-end flow: listing -> dynamic pricing -> claim -> optional delivery.
- Practical for local adoption by street-food and small vendors.


8) Demo Script (2-3 Minutes)
1. Open guest marketplace and show live decreasing prices.
2. Sign in with Google.
3. As vendor: add a perishable item.
4. As buyer: claim item with pickup or delivery.
5. Show delivery fee quote using distance.
6. Open delivery map and claim a nearby delivery task.
7. Conclude with impact: less waste, faster sell-through, cheaper food.


9) Impact Metrics to Mention
- Waste reduction: unsold perishables sold before expiry.
- Vendor benefit: improved recovery from end-of-day stock.
- Buyer benefit: lower prices on quality food.
- Community effect: local micro-delivery earning opportunity.


10) Business / Future Scope
- Smart demand forecasting and auto-listing suggestions.
- Reputation and trust scoring for vendors/deliverers.
- Route optimization for multi-drop deliveries.
- Local language support.
- Merchant analytics dashboard and subscription model.
